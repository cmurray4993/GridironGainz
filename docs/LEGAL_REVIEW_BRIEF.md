# Gridiron Gainz legal review brief

## Product summary

Gridiron Gainz is a fictional American-football manager and collectible-card web game. Players open randomized packs, build seven-player offense and defense lineups plus special teams, earn non-transferable Coins from active-lineup fans, play server-simulated seasons, and trade off-chain cards in an Auction House. A connected Solana wallet is optional during beta.

## Current closed-beta design

- Adults only; U.S.-limited enrollment control.
- Solana devnet only; no asset or reward has cash value.
- Email/password accounts; public wallets only, no private-key custody.
- Coins, GC, cards, packs, lineups, seasons, auctions, and ledgers stored in Supabase.
- Server verifies pack costs, generates results, creates uniquely identified cards, and records odds version.
- Server assigns schedules, locks lineups, simulates official games, and records results.
- Test GC purchases are separated from contest rewards. The previous automatic 60/20/20 allocation is disabled.
- SOL marketplace settlement is devnet-only and independently launch-locked.

## Possible future features requiring advice

- Selling GC for mainnet SOL.
- Using GC or Coins to open randomized packs.
- Letting pack contents improve teams in a skill-based competition.
- Awarding sponsor-funded SOL prizes.
- Peer-to-peer auctions with Coin bids, Coin Buy Now, or SOL Buy Now.
- Fixed marketplace or transaction fees.
- Operation through wallet browsers such as Phantom or Solflare.

## Questions for gaming/promotions counsel

1. In which jurisdictions can the proposed season be a lawful skill contest?
2. Does a free route eliminate consideration when paid packs can improve competitive strength?
3. Should real-prize play use standardized, salary-capped, or separately earned cards?
4. Which locations require exclusion, registration, bonding, free-entry language, age restrictions, or winner filings?
5. What official-rules, advertising, odds, prize-value, winner-verification, and tax disclosures are required?
6. How should randomized paid packs be structured or restricted?

## Questions for financial-regulation counsel

1. Does facilitating direct buyer-to-seller SOL settlement create money-transmission, virtual-currency, broker, marketplace, sanctions, KYC, AML, or reporting obligations?
2. Does the answer change if the operator never takes custody, charges no fee, or limits trading to Coins?
3. What location, identity, sanctions, monitoring, recordkeeping, and suspicious-activity controls are needed?

## Questions for privacy/consumer counsel

1. Are the age, location, consent, privacy, refund, and randomized-content disclosures adequate?
2. Which state privacy notices and request workflows are needed?
3. What retention periods apply to accounts, wallets, transactions, contest results, winners, disputes, and security logs?

## Required deliverable

Ask counsel for written launch conditions, approved/excluded jurisdictions, document changes, registration/filing requirements, and a clear answer on whether the paid-pack + prize and SOL-marketplace designs may launch. Verbal reassurance is not enough for enabling the mainnet flags.
